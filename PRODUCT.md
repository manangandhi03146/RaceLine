# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Street and track day riders who record rides with GPS and motion data, and moto content creators who want to turn their sessions into polished, shareable social cards. They check the app briefly before a session starts, glance at live stats while off-bike, and spend real time in the Share and Garage tabs after the ride. The phone might be in a sweaty jacket pocket or propped on a paddock table.

## Product Purpose

MotorcycleTrackShare records motorcycle rides (GPS route, speed, lean angle, elapsed time) and converts that raw data into social-ready share cards. Secondary surfaces include a bike Garage and a Calendar for reviewing past sessions. Success looks like a rider finishing a track day and posting a share card they're genuinely proud of within two minutes of stopping.

## Brand Personality

Bold, social, expressive. This is a tool with personality, not a utility that happens to have a UI. Voice is confident and direct, never clinical. Think: the rider who has great taste, not the engineer who optimized everything.

## Anti-references

- **Hardcore motorsport/racing aesthetics**: No carbon-fiber textures, no neon-on-black helmet-cam overlays, no aggressive racing-game energy. Too one-note.
- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: No hero-metric card grids, no sidebar navigation, no B2B data-table aesthetics. This is a personal tool, not an analytics platform.

## Design Principles

1. **Ride story over raw data.** Numbers are supporting cast. The route, the bike, the moment -- that's the headline. Design for narrative, not spreadsheet.
2. **Share-ready is first-class.** The share card is the product's signature output. Every design decision should make the end card feel worth posting.
3. **Bold but considered.** High visual energy that reads as personal and confident, not aggressive or noisy. A well-chosen jacket, not a racing suit.
4. **Personality without performance.** Expressive design doesn't mean heavy animation or gradient overload. It means every surface has a point of view.
5. **Glanceable by default.** Text hierarchy must work at arm's length. Riders don't read; they scan.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard iOS baseline: Dynamic Type and Dark Mode support. High contrast between UI text and backgrounds matters because the app is used in variable outdoor lighting conditions.
